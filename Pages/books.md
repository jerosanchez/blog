# Books

Last update: **August 2020**

This is the list of books that make up **my "library fund" as a professional programmer**. I have read many others, but they are not on the list because they do not seem to me to be fundamental. And I haven't read some of those that are included, but they are included because I consider they are a must read.

I do not include books on specific technologies or programming languages, unless they are **relevant to the entire community** as a whole. For example, Git, which has already become the _de facto_ standard for version control. Or the mythical "The C programming language", because most modern languages ​​owe a lot to this language (just like Lisp).

It is a living list, in which I am including and removing items according to the progress of our profession or what I discover. I also try to review those that I am reading or rereading, to offer a personal vision of each one.

Although many have a Spanish translation, I have preferred to list the original editions in English. A teacher of mine used to say that **the first language a programmer should learn is the English language**. For better or worse, the vast majority of good technical literature is originally written in the language of Shakespeare. And in my experience, many important subtleties are lost during the translation process, when not you are directly encountering mediocre translations.

Lastly, don't be fooled by the age of some of the books. They are on this list for good reason. **The best programmer is not the one who knows the latest framework or the fashionable tool, but the one who understands the subtleties of our profession, its principles and best practices**, which in many cases have been with us for longer than we think. Therefore, nothing better than "listening" to our elders.
